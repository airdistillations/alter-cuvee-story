03
IV The industry's shortcut

What the market offers
isn't fit for your table.
Not even close.

Most non-alcoholic wine is built the wrong way. It is dismantled, then re-painted. Would you send that out of your kitchen?

Step 01

Poor wine made

Low-grade wine is produced at scale — the base is already compromised before processing begins.

Step 02

Alcohol stripped

Industrial vacuum distillation or reverse osmosis strips out the alcohol — and with it all the esters, flavonoids and aromatics that made it worth drinking.

Step 03

Re-aromatized

The flavourless liquid is rebuilt with artificial flavourings and preservatives. A ghost of wine — not a gastronomic choice.

The greatest respect one can give to wine is not to pretend to be it. De-alcoholization is the most disrespectful, betrayed interpretation of a non-alcoholic alternative — and your guests can taste it.

04
V Our answer

We don't tear down.
We build.

Old school meets new school. Two real stories in one bottle.

ALTER works directly with winegrowers. We ask them to harvest earlier — verjus, the unripe grape — a juice that still holds the full flavour signature of its variety, with natural acidity and a beautiful sweet-sour balance. The soul of the terroir, intact.

On that viticultural platform we build a second act: our own distillates from self-cultivated herbs, flowers and botanicals. A winemaker's nose, palate and finish — composed with intention, not imitated from a bottle being dismantled.

05
VI The ALTER method

Field to bottle,
in three acts.

01

Verjus — the platform

Early-harvest grapes, picked with our winegrowers before full ripeness. This juice carries the natural sour, sweet and bitter balance of the cuvée — the terroir foundation we build everything on.

02

Botanical distillation — the palate

Herbs, flowers and botanicals from our own cultivation, distilled in-house. These non-alcoholic distillates define the flavour palate — adding an adult depth of complexity to the grape platform.

03

Blending — the cuvée

We blend the two acts the way a winemaker assembles cépages — layering so the cuvée opens in three distinct phases: nose, palate, finish. The structure a sommelier can work with.

VII The language of wine

The same ritual.
A different story.

ALTER doesn't chase the flavour of wine — that is dealcoholization's mistake. ALTER builds the ritual of wine: the three-act tasting arc every sommelier recognises and every guest instinctively reads.

Pour, swirl, nose, sip, finish. Discuss. We speak the same language as your wine list — primary, secondary and tertiary aromas, unfolding from nose to palate to finish. We just taste different. And that's exactly the point.
